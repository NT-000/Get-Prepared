Baked Chicken Schnitzel

Oven-baked chicken schnitzel that’s golden and crisp outside, tender and juicy inside. Less oil, easier cleanup. Serve with lemon wedges and a German-style potato salad.

Yield: ~12 schnitzels (serves 6–8) Prep: ~15 min Bake: ~18–22 min Oven: 425°F / 220°C

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, or as desired
  • 6 chicken breasts, cut in half lengthwise (butterflied)
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon ground paprika
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups seasoned bread crumbs
  • 1 large lemon, zested

Tip: Pat chicken dry for extra-crispy crumbs. For even browning, bake on a wire rack set over the sheet pan.

Instructions

  1. Preheat & prep pan. Heat oven to 425°F / 220°C. Brush a rimmed baking sheet (or a rack set over it) with olive oil.
  2. Season chicken. Pat the butterflied chicken dry. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  3. Set up breading station.
    • Shallow bowl 1: flour + paprika (whisk to combine).
    • Shallow bowl 2: beaten eggs.
    • Shallow bowl 3: seasoned bread crumbs mixed with the lemon zest.
  4. Bread chicken. Dredge each piece in flour (shake off excess), dip in egg, then press into bread crumbs to coat thoroughly. Let rest 5 minutes so the coating adheres.
  5. Arrange & oil. Place schnitzels on the prepared sheet (or rack). Drizzle or lightly brush tops with a little more olive oil.
  6. Bake. Bake for 10–12 minutes, then flip and bake another 8–10 minutes, until golden and the thickest part reaches 165°F / 74°C.
  7. Optional crunch boost. For deeper color, switch to broil/grill for 1–2 minutes at the end—watch closely.
  8. Serve. Rest 3 minutes. Serve with lemon wedges and German-style potato salad.
Note: Time may vary with cutlet thickness and oven. Aim for safe internal temp of 165°F / 74°C.
