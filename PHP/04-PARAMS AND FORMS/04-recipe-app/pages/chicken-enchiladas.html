Creamy Chicken Enchiladas

Easy tortillas stuffed with a savory, mildly spiced chicken & cheese filling. The secret’s in the seasonings! Top with sour cream and serve with Mexican rice.

Serves: 5 (one 12" tortilla each) Prep: ~20 min Cook: ~25 min Oven: 350°F / 175°C

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken breasts, cut into chunks
  • 1 ¼ cups sour cream
  • 1 (10.5 ounce) can condensed cream of chicken soup
  • ¼ teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 bunch green onions, chopped, divided
  • 1 (4 ounce) can chopped green chiles, drained
  • 1 (1.25 ounce) package mild taco seasoning mix
  • 1 teaspoon lime juice
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • 5 (12-inch) flour tortillas
  • 3 cups shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 1 (10 ounce) can enchilada sauce
  • 1 (6 ounce) can sliced black olives, drained

Tip: Reserve some green onions for garnish. If you like extra sauce, serve with a little more sour cream on the side.

Instructions

  1. Preheat. Heat oven to 350°F / 175°C. Lightly grease a 9×13 inch (23×33 cm) baking dish.
  2. Cook the chicken. In a large skillet over medium heat, melt butter. Sauté the chopped onion 2–3 min until softened. Add the chicken chunks; cook until no longer pink.
  3. Season & simmer. Stir in the taco seasoning and water. Simmer 2–3 min to thicken. Stir in the green chiles and about half of the green onions. Remove from heat.
  4. Make the creamy sauce. In a bowl, whisk together sour cream, cream of chicken soup, chili powder, onion powder, garlic powder, and lime juice until smooth.
  5. Moisten the filling (optional but tasty). Fold about ½ cup of the creamy sauce into the chicken mixture to make it extra luscious.
  6. Assemble. Warm the tortillas briefly so they’re pliable. Divide the chicken mixture among the tortillas; add about 2 cups of the Cheddar (leaving 1 cup for topping). Roll up and place seam-side down in the baking dish.
  7. Sauce & top. Pour the enchilada sauce evenly over the rolls, then spoon the remaining creamy sauce on top. Sprinkle with the remaining Cheddar and the black olives.
  8. Bake. Bake for 20–25 minutes until bubbly and heated through. Rest 5 minutes. Garnish with remaining green onions. Serve with Mexican rice and a dollop of sour cream, if you like.
Note: Quantities are exactly as provided. Baking temperature/time are standard for enchiladas; adjust to your oven.
